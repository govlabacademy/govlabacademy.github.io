  • Working in or thinking about starting an analytics unit within your organization?

Course Description

Today’s society generates data at an astounding rate. Growing almost as quickly are expectations about how such data could be used and how much value it could create. Yet there are challenges to deriving useful and actionable insights from big data and translating those into new policies, effective services and better ways to improve the lives of people in our communities. This program will focus on how to design, implement, and evaluate analytics projects so that they consistently deliver measurable, scalable results. We will talk about how to identify tested frameworks for data diagnostics, acquisition, and sampling; discuss strategies for framing complex problems so they can be legitimately addressed by analytics work; and work out practical guidelines for working responsibly with data-driven insight to the end of advancing your projects. There is a special focus on the practical aspects of creating actionable insight in urban environments and empowering you to contribute to making cities more efficient, livable, equitable, and resilient. This program is designed for leaders, executives, and entrepreneurs who want to be on the cutting edge of the big data revolution. It will benefit professionals from the public and private sectors involved in all city-related industries.

Course Format

1 faculty-led session every week for 2 hours (one hour is with a guest expert and one hour is an all-group session); and a personalized schedule of peer-to-peer and one-on-one coaching sessions.

Course Duration: 8 weeks

Faculty Members

Amen Ra Mashariki

Chief Analytics Officer of New York City

Amen Ra, former White House Fellow and currently Chief Analytics Officer for the New York City Mayor's Office of Data Analytics, is a computer scientist at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory (JHU APL). As a senior bioinformatics researcher at JHU APL he develops and utilizes smart algorithms that explore biological data in search of complex associations and relationships in order to provide insight into common biological diagnostics and clinical trends. Prior to academia, Amen has spent 7 years in the technical industry as a senior software engineer for Motorola. He has authored 5 patent disclosures, and received the prestigious Chicago Museum of Science ‘Top Technology Innovators’ award. Over the last 8 years Amen has spent his summers teaching advanced computer science courses for 7th – 10th graders at the Johns Hopkins University Center For Talented Youth program. Amen earned his doctorate degree from Morgan State University, his Master’s degree in computer science from Howard University, and also his bachelor’s degree in computer science from Lincoln University.
